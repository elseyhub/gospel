Who We Are

Gospel Christian Ministry started in a living room in 1987 with nine people and a coffee pot. We're bigger now, but that's still roughly the spirit of it — a place where the conversation matters as much as the sermon, and where it's fine to show up with more questions than answers.

We're a Christian church rooted in scripture, shaped by an old fishing town, and genuinely glad when someone walks in who's never done this before. Come in jeans. Bring your kids, your skepticism, or both.

Pastor Elena Marsh leads our congregation alongside a team of elders, and most Sundays you'll find one of them at the coffee table before service — happy to answer anything.

Service Times

  • Sunday, 9:00 AM — Early service. Quieter and smaller, with a slower pace.
  • Sunday, 11:00 AM — Our fullest gathering, with the whole band and a packed kids' program. Coffee and pastries out front from 10:30.
  • Wednesday, 6:30 PM — Bible study in the fellowship hall, currently working through the Gospel of Luke.

First Time? Here's What to Expect

  1. Park anywhere on Harbor Road. A greeter in a blue vest will point you toward the front doors.
  2. Grab coffee, sit anywhere. Seating is open and unassigned.
  3. Kids check-in is at the side door. Ages 0–10 have their own program during the 11:00 service.
  4. Service runs about 70 minutes. Singing, a message from Pastor Elena, and time to stay and talk afterward.

Ministries

Harbor Kids

Sunday programming for ages 0–10, plus a Thursday after-school club during the school year.

Student Ministry

Grades 6–12 meet Friday nights for games, discussion, and the occasional lock-in.

Worship Team

Musicians and vocalists rehearse Thursday evenings — auditions are informal, just come play.

Small Groups

About twenty groups meet in homes across town on weeknights — we'll help you find one nearby.

Outreach

Monthly meal service at the county shelter, plus a winter coat drive every November.

Local Missions

A standing partnership with two shelters and a food pantry — volunteers always needed.
